Hotline:

CyberEthics Hotline promotes safe use of the Internet in Cyprus. It serves the needs of all people living on the island (eg Turkish Cypriots and other minorities) and focuses on child pornography, child eroticism, child nudity, child sex abuse, child trafficking, child sex tourism, racism, racial discrimination Inappropriate use of photos that do not belong to the same users.
It works together as an Information Node and Complaint Line called CyberEthics. The project involves governmental and social actors, thus contributing to the elimination of cybercrime through action by European citizens and public institutions aimed at changing human behavior, attitudes and attitudes, with particular emphasis on rural and less developed regions Island's.

Hotline aims

•To operate and coordinate on a pan-Cypriot basis the Complaint Line through which internet users in Cyprus can report illegal and harmful content and activities in order to reduce the trafficking of illegal content on the Internet.
• Inform users of the Internet for the purposes of the Complaint Line and the ways of communicating with the Line. To clarify to users the difference between their own activities and those public authorities, informing them of the existence of alternative ways of reporting illegal content.
• Directly handle complaints arriving at the Line in accordance with the best practice guidelines established by the Network and in co-operation with law enforcement authorities
• Exchange specific information about the detected illegal content with other network members.
• Develop a structured method of consultation with relevant stakeholders (eg internet providers, media regulator, judicial authorities) in cooperation with other countries' Safe Networks.
